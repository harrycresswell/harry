> “Thursday, May 21, 2026, marks the 15th Global Accessibility Awareness Day (GAAD). The purpose of GAAD is to get everyone talking, thinking and learning about digital access/inclusion and people with different disabilities.”

The perfect occasion to share a handful of my favourite web accessibility resources:

- [How A Screen Reader User Surfs The Web](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OUDV1gqs9GA). Léonie Watson demonstrates how a blind screen reader user explores the web and reveals how semantic HTML has a huge impact on both accessibility and performance.
- [Accessibility for designer: where do I start?](https://stephaniewalter.design/blog/accessibility-resources-tools-articles-books-for-designer/). Stephanie Walter’s definitive list of articles, resources, checklists, tools, plugins and books to help you design accessible digital products.
- [Accessibility Developer Guide](https://www.accessibility-developer-guide.com/). A fantastic how-to guide on designing and implementing fully accessible websites.
- [The WebAIM Million](https://webaim.org/projects/million/). The 2026 accessibility evaluation of the home pages for the top 1,000,000 web sites.
- [Accessibility Myths](https://a11ymyths.com/). 22 accessibility myths debunked.
