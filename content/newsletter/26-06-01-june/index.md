The UK summer has arrived. Usually that’s my cue to slow down and enjoy the good weather. But with so much uncertainty about where our industry is heading, it somehow feels difficult to justify this time around.

Personally, I’m doing my best to shut out the noise, stick to the plan and do what I’m doing slowly and intentionally. Whether this is a good bet or not is yet to be seen, but at least I’ll look back knowing I was doing good, honest work, for people who care.

As per usual, here are my highlights from the past month, a few good links from around the web and what I have planned for the month ahead.

## Highlights from last month

Celebrated a couple of important birthdays. First my dads with lunch at one of our favourite local pubs. Then the 40th of my university housemate with a spot of bowling at Rowans in Finsbury Park.

Watched [my good friend Matt](/writing/designing-morpho-album-artwork/) play live to a packed crowd at No90 for [Queens Yard Summer Party](https://queensyardsummer.party/) in Hackney Wick.

Myself, R and a friend who was over from Hamburg, made the trip over to the Natural History Museum for the [Wildlife photographer of the year](https://www.nhm.ac.uk/visit/exhibitions/wildlife-photographer-of-the-year.html) exhibition.

Ran the [Hoka Hackney Half](https://www.hackneymoves.com/half-marathon) in perfect running conditions. Though we were so caught up in the moment we failed to get any good pictures.

Celebrated the wedding of one of R’s good friends at St Mary of Eton church, followed by a great cocktail bar in Hornsey called [Little Mercies](https://littlemercies.co.uk/).

Published [Global Accessibility Awareness Day](/links/global-accessibility-awareness-day/) and [an update to my /now page](/now/26-05-21/).

Pushed a new release for [Mood, my mood boarding Hugo theme](https://github.com/harrycresswell/mood/releases/tag/v0.4.0), which brings templates inline with Hugo’s new-ish templating system, and adds some fun pixelated LQIP’s using the `image-rendering` property.

## Elsewhere on the web

[Intent is the design superpower AI can't replace](https://somedesigners.substack.com/p/intent-is-the-design-superpower-ai) from Raff Di Meo.

> ”What’s not democratised, what AI genuinely cannot do, is understanding. Knowing which problem is actually worth solving. Knowing how users think about a product, where they get stuck, and what they’re really trying to do underneath the surface request. Knowing why a solution will land or fall flat before you’ve spent two weeks building it.
> 
> That’s the job. It has always been.”

[Don't put aria-label on generic elements like divs](https://www.matuzo.at/blog/2026/aria-label-generic-elements) from Manuel Matuzović.

Manuel explains the reasons why adding `aria-label` to generic element isn’t a great idea. There are some exceptions, of course.

 [An interview with UX researcher and Accessible Product Designer Stéphanie Walter](https://davesmyth.com/interviews/stephanie-walter) from Dave Symth.
 
 Dave’s latest interview with Stéphanie Walter is well worth a read and packed full of great tips about building enterprise tools, products and services that employees use on a daily basis.

[The Case for Design Disposables](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/design-disposables/) from Laura Klein.

> ”Before you start making anything, ask yourself: Who is this for?
> 
> If the answer is "for me, so I can think through this problem," you're making a disposable. Keep it rough. Keep it fast. Give yourself permission to be wrong. The goal is to get clarity, not to produce something impressive.
> 
> If the answer is "for someone else, so they can understand my work," you're making a deliverable. Take the time to do it well. Format it. Annotate it. Make sure it communicates clearly to someone who wasn't in your head when you made it.

[How to add icons to external links with CSS](https://gomakethings.com/articles/how-to-add-icons-to-external-links-with-css/) from Chris Ferdinandi.

This is a very handy bit of CSS. Thank you Chris!

[AI Doesn't Fix Accessible Systems. It Depends on Them.](https://annaecook.com/writing/2026/ai-doesnt-fix-accessible-systems-it-depends-on-them) from Anna E. Cook.

> ”The evidence is now in the data. [WebAIM's 2026 Million report](https://webaim.org/projects/million/) shows the first reversal in six years of accessibility progress — **95.9% of homepages now fail WCAG**, errors per page jumped 10.1% in a single year, and page complexity is up 22.5% — **all closely tracking the rise of AI-assisted coding.**
> 
> The real work is the same work it has always been: build systems that hold up for [real people in real conditions](https://annaecook.com/writing/2026/1/5/designing-accessibility-for-real-use-not-dashboards), and govern them so the structure does not drift. **AI does not change that work. It depends on it.**”

One of two brilliant companion articles to Anna’s recent [axe-con design track keynote](https://www.deque.com/axe-con/sessions/accessibility-in-the-end-of-deterministic-design-again/). Well worth a read.

[Reach out and say thanks](https://www.kevinpowell.co/article/tell-someone-you-appreciate-them/) from Kevin Powell.

Great piece from Kevin, which highlights why it’s now more important than even to share baseline educational content. Even though creating it can feel like a pointless exercise. And if you value what other creators are making, then make sure you let them know, to encourge them to keep creating it.
