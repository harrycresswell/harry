## Elsewhere on the web

[Write-first design](https://karlkoch.me/writing/write-first-design) from Karl Koch.

“A paragraph cannot hide. Write "we believe this change will improve clarity, because users currently misread the loading state", and every part of that sentence is now arguable. Someone can challenge the belief, or the evidence for it. That is the point. You want the argument exposed while it is still cheap to change.”

Karl makes a solid argument for why writing should come first in the design process, then explains how it works in practice with a sequence of written artefacts.

[The hidden costs of LLM problem-solving](https://davesmyth.com/hidden-costs) from Dave Smyth.

Dave highlights some of the ways AI is affecting the collaborative process, explaining why it’s not a great idea to approach experts with prescribed AI generated solutions. He also offers suggestions for how to frame conversations for a much more constructive outcome. 

[Printing Ordinal Numbers in Hugo](https://wilhelm.codes/blog/printing-ordinal-numbers-in-hugo/) from Wilhelm Murdoch.

I stumbled across this useful post whilst catching up with the latest happens on the Hugo community Discourse. Wilhelm is doing some really cool things with Hugo, which he documents over on [his personal site](https://wilhelm.codes/tags/hugo/).

[Do I Have to Post More on Social Media to Grow My Agency?](https://danmall.com/learn/the-business-of-design/do-i-have-to-post-more-on-social-media-to-grow-my-agency) from Dan Mall.

“The strongest marketing I know for agency owners is to make a list of 100 potential clients and send each one a well-written pitch.”

Good advise as always from Dan.

[The Web is for People](https://www.torgo.com/blog/2026/06/the-web-is-for-people.html) from Daniel Appelquist.

“The web is a philosophical choice to build a system that puts humans as its core constituency. The platform of technologies designed with that philosophy in mind may include AI-related systems. But if they are going to be part of the web, then they must be designed to put humans first.”

A timely reminder from Daniel that human beings are the number one priority in the [W3C’s Web Platform Design Principles](https://www.w3.org/TR/design-principles/#priority-of-constituencies).

[How building an HTML-first site doubled our users overnight](https://www.mohkohn.co.uk/writing/html-first/) from Alistair Davidson.

“It is not acceptable to bounce users on old browsers, users with bad network connections, users using assistive technologies. Certainly not from a monopoly public service. A lot of hype and noise is pressing us to extend the cowboy, wild-west phase of the software industry’s expansion. We should set that aside, and take ourselves seriously as a mature industry. Build a web application that works on a playstation portable on a 3G connection - if you do, it will work for all your users, and it will still work 30 years from now.”

A great case study for building progressively-enhanced websites that work great for everyone.
