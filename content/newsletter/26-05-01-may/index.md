It should hit 24°C in London today. I feel fortunate to have a garden right now. It feels good to swing the doors wide open and listen to the birds chirping, while I punch away at my keyboard.

Typically, I also have a cold. I’m seeing it as a good excuse to finish up early, head to the park and enjoy some time in the sunshine, away from a screen. Work can wait for another day.

As per usual, here are my highlights from the past month, a few good links from around the web and what I have planned for the month ahead.

## Highlights from last month

Made [a bunch of improvements to cu.css](https://cu.harrycresswell.com/changelog/v0.2.0/). Mostly theme related and re-writing parts of the documetation website.

Published [Designing the Morpho album artwork](/writing/designing-morpho-album-artwork/). This one’s a little different and nothing to do with building websites. It’s about creating music artwork for a 12 inch vinyl release.

Spent time in Rabel with R’s family, celebrating her parents birthdays. Saw friends in Hamburg and Mainz.

Enjoyed a few longer runs. In particular, one from Rabel along the coast to Maasholm, finishing up on the beach in Hasselberg. Also, the Alster lake loop in Hamburg. And, of course, the classic one from home to Greenwich, then along the Thames path down to Borough Market.

Discovered 150ml soft flasks and home made gels.

## Elsewhere on the web

[Output isn’t design](https://linear.app/now/output-isn-t-design) from Karri Saarinen

> “Working visually keeps me close to the problem and is slow enough [that it] gives me time to think while I work. Moving things around, testing relationships, and refining structure is not separate from the thinking. It is part of how clarity emerges.”

[Good designers, bad websites: a proposal](https://alistapart.com/article/good-designers-bad-websites-a-proposal/) from Alan Dalton.

Why do some designs still exclude people? Alan has some insight and explains how to use personas and guidelines to recognise accessibility issues while you design.

[Email address obfuscation: What works in 2026?](https://spencermortensen.com/articles/email-obfuscation/).

Spencer Mortensen shares best techniques for keeping email addresses hidden from spammers—along with the statistics on how likely they are to be broken.

[Inverted themes with light-dark()](https://daverupert.com/2026/04/inverted-light-dark/) from Dave Rupert.

Dave demonstrates how `light-dark()` makes it trivial to support dual color modes in a single stylesheet, without adding too much weight.

[Thoughts on slowing the fuck down](https://mariozechner.at/posts/2026-03-25-thoughts-on-slowing-the-fuck-down/) from Mario Zechner.

> “Give yourself time to think about what you're actually building and why. Give yourself an opportunity to say, fuck no, we don't need this. Set yourself limits on how much code you let the clanker generate per day, in line with your ability to actually review the code
> 
> ...slowing the fuck down and suffering some friction is what allows you to learn and grow.”

[6 Questions I Ask Every Week to Keep My Life Genuinely Simple](https://nosidebar.com/6-questions-i-ask-every-week/) from Jyoti Yadav.

I’m sure Jyoti’s story will resonate with many. I, for one, feel drawn to keeping life simple the more complicated it seems to get. Particularly when it comes to work. These 6 questions will certainly get you thinking.
