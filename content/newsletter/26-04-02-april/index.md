I’m gearing up for a DIY-filled Easter bank holiday weekend here in London.

Finally getting round to laying the flooring that’s been sitting in our hallway for what feels like forever now. Time to rip up our nasty brown office carpet and get the power tools out. I’m looking forward to getting to work.

As per usual, here are my highlights from the past month, a few good links from around the web and what I have planned for the month ahead.

## Highlights from last month

Spent four wonderfully sunny days skiing in the [Haute-Savoie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haute-Savoie) region of France. We explored Praz-sur-Arly, Megève, Crest-Voland and Les Saisies.

Made a few updates to practicalhugo.com: added [video facades](https://practicalhugo.com/changelog/2026/03/06/video-facades/), a [terms of use](https://practicalhugo.com/changelog/2026/03/03/terms-of-use/) page and made some [important accessibility improvements](https://practicalhugo.com/changelog/2026/03/11/image-video-accessibility/).

Improved the theming capabilities of [cu.css](https://cu.harrycresswell.com/). Hoping to push these updates along with documentation later this month. 

Published a quick primer on [HTML elements vs tags](https://harrycresswell.com/writing/html-elements-vs-tags/), because it’s easy to forget the difference sometimes.

Watched [Project Hail Mary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Project_Hail_Mary_(film)) at the BFI IMAX and absolutely loved it. I had high hopes having enjoyed the book so much, so it was great to see them get the film spot on. Go see it if you haven’t already.

## Elsewhere on the web

[Offline 23 hours a day](https://sive.rs/off23) from Derek Sivers.

> “I notice it most at the start of the day. No news. No texts. I have no idea what’s going on out there, so I develop what’s going on **in here**. My writing, coding, and learning fills the time and space...My thoughts feel more independent. I explore my own ideas deeper before looking for other perspectives.”

Derek’s new life reminds me a little of Henry David Thoreau’s Walden. But more so how important it is to disconnect, spend some time offline, and get outside.

[When All You Can Do Is All or Nothing, Do Nothing](https://csswizardry.com/2026/03/when-all-you-can-do-is-all-or-nothing-do-nothing/) from Harry Roberts.

> “Sometimes, at design system level, the least clever option is the safest. If all you can do is lazy-load everything or lazy-load nothing, choose nothing; if all you can do is mark several possible winners as high priority, choose none.”

A lot has been written about how detrimental incorrect use of ARIA can be, so it’s great to see Harry sharing similar wisdom regarding the use of performance hints.

[Applying accessibility fixes with stealth for the greater good](https://piccalil.li/blog/applying-accessibility-fixes-with-stealth-for-the-greater-good/) from Steve Frenzel.

A fantastic read on the general state of accessibility in the tech industry and how to sneak it into your organisation when it’s not considered a priority.

[The Color System I Wish I Understood Sooner](https://theadminbar.com/semantics-and-primitives-color-system) from Kyle Van Deusen. 

A great introduction to primitive and semantic tokens, where they fall short, and how to combine the two to create a robust colour system. 

I’m trying to avoiding reading too much about AI these days. Quite frankly I’m sick of it. A couple of posts this month did speak to me though, so here they are.

[Dealing with AI burnout](https://www.alexhyett.com/newsletter/dealing-with-ai-burnout/) from Alex Hyett.

> “As always, technological advancements aren't giving us back time they are demanding more out of our working hours to the point of burnout.”

[Making and Machines](https://www.chrbutler.com/making-and-machines) from Christopher Butler.

> “AI is capable of mimicking the output of any artist, of that I am sure. But it cannot give an artist what she gets by making herself. And _this_—this irreducible value in the act of making—is why craft will persist.”
