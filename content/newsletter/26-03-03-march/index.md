It’s feeling a lot like Spring already here in London. After all the rain we’ve had this year, a few sunny days and some warmer weather is a welcomed change.

I spent a good part of the weekend in the garden. Hacking back the buddleia and getting the beds in check. Finally feeling on top of things for once. I’m sure it won’t last long, but I’ll enjoy it whilst it does.

As per usual, here are my highlights from the past month, a few good links from around the web and what I have planned for the month ahead.

## Highlights from last month

Spent time with family and friends in the [Stubai Valley](https://www.stubai.at/en/stubaital/villages-of-the-stubai-valley/), Austria. We skied [Schlick 2000](https://www.stubai.at/en/skiing-resorts/schlick2000/) and [Stubai Gletscher](https://www.stubaier-gletscher.com/en/) in a good mix of conditions. Stunning landscape, nice wide open pistes, not too busy. Couldn’t have asked for much more. Here I am [enjoying the powder](/now/26-02-13/) on the final day.

[Practical Hugo](https://practicalhugo.com/) is now live! This project been a long time coming, so I’m super excited to finally get the first season out there.

Finished watching [Spielberg’s 2002 mini-series Taken](https://youtube.com/playlist?list=PLXkeH6bNuCQ6ds_nTlmkpqrPKCCJgb9Ym&si=0tQQWiMRW1q3qEW1). Great show for its time and free to watch on YouTube.

## Elsewhere on the web

[If you treat every day like a feast, soon even the finest wine will taste like water](https://www.lifeafterthedailygrind.com/p/if-you-treat-every-day-like-a-feast) from Life after the daily grind.

> The mistake is not enjoying comfort or pleasure, but normalising them. When everything is designed to please us, nothing rewards us. By allowing space for effort, inconvenience, and moments we would rather avoid, we preserve our capacity for joy. Life does not need to be relentlessly improved. It needs to fluctuate.

[The return of the intuitive designer in the age of AI](https://uxdesign.cc/the-return-of-the-intuitive-designer-in-the-age-of-ai-6f0ea728d1d0) from James Harrison 

James writes about staying relevant by honing your intuition, not your process.

[Build notes for the new 16by9 site](https://marcjenkins.co.uk/build-notes-16by9/) from Marc Jenkins.

I enjoyed reading about how Marc approached the rebrand and repositioning of his design business. The Website Redesign Handbook is a fantastic idea and no doubt a great way to qualify and educate prospective clients.

[Big Design, Bold Ideas](https://dbushell.com/2026/02/09/big-design-and-bold-ideas/) from David Bushell. 

Another great write up on a website redesign. David has built an interactive game embedded in the homepage to help reinforce his stance on AI. Brilliant stuff.

[role=presentation is no alternative for aria-hidden=true](https://www.matuzo.at/blog/2026/role-presentation-no-alternative-for-aria-hidden) from Manuel Matuzović.

Useful accessibility tips on how to successfully hide presentational SVGs. I’m currently reading Manuel’s book [Web Accessibility Cookbook](https://accessibility-cookbook.com/), which I highly recommend.

[Figcaptions versus alt text](https://www.tempertemper.net/blog/figcaptions-versus-alt-text) from Martin Underhill.

Martin explains the difference between figcaptions and alternative text. Spoiler: alt text should describe the image, whereas figcaption should be used to explain what part the image plays in whatever you’re writing.
